Balut

Recipe

  • Ingredients: Duck eggs, water, salt
  • Step 1: Select fertilized duck eggs that are about 16-18 days old.
  • Step 2: Place the eggs in a pot and cover with water. Add a tablespoon of salt.
  • Step 3: Bring to a boil and simmer for 30 minutes.
  • Step 4: Remove from heat and let cool.
  • Step 5: Serve with a pinch of salt, chili, and vinegar.

History of Balut

Balut, a popular street food in the Philippines, is a fertilized duck egg with a partially developed embryo. It's known for its unique taste and texture.

The origins of balut are unclear, but it's believed to have been introduced by Chinese traders in the Philippines centuries ago. It's become a symbol of Filipino culture and is often enjoyed with a pinch of salt and a splash of vinegar.

Though controversial for some, balut is celebrated by many as a nutritious and flavorful delicacy, often enjoyed during social gatherings.
